Kheer(Desert)

Description

Kheer is a beloved Indian sweet dish made from rice, milk, and sugar, often flavored with cardamom, and garnished with nuts and dried fruits. It’s a creamy, comforting dessert that’s enjoyed across the Indian subcontinent and is commonly served during festivals, special occasions, or as a simple treat.

Ingredients

  • Rice (basmati or short-grain)
  • Milk (full-fat)
  • Sugar
  • Cardamom pods (or ground cardamom)
  • Saffron strands (optional)
  • Almonds (chopped or whole)
  • Cashews
  • Pistachios
  • Raisins
  • Cherries (optional)

Steps

  1. Rinse the rice thoroughly under cold water to remove excess starch.
  2. In a large pan, bring the milk to a boil over medium heat.
  3. Add the rinsed rice to the boiling milk. Reduce the heat to low and simmer, stirring occasionally.
  4. Cook the rice in the milk until it becomes soft and the milk thickens, about 30-40 minutes.
  5. Add sugar to the mixture and stir until it dissolves completely.
  6. Add crushed cardamom pods (or ground cardamom) and saffron strands (if using). Stir well to combine.
  7. Simmer the kheer for an additional 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  8. Remove the pan from heat and let the kheer cool slightly.
  9. Garnish with chopped almonds, cashews, pistachios, raisins, and cherries (if using).
  10. Serve warm or chilled, according to your preference.
