Chicken Biriyani (Spicy, Masaledar)

Description

Chicken biryani is a flavorful and aromatic South Asian rice dish that blends tender chicken with fragrant spices and basmati rice. Originating from the Indian subcontinent, this dish is known for its rich, complex flavors and vibrant colors.

Ingredients

  • Chicken (cut into pieces)
  • Yogurt
  • Turmeric powder
  • Cumin powder
  • Coriander powder
  • Garam masala
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Salt
  • Basmati rice
  • Onions (sliced thinly)
  • Tomatoes (chopped)
  • Fresh cilantro (chopped)
  • Fresh mint leaves (chopped)
  • Fried onions
  • Saffron strands (optional)
  • Oil or ghee
  • Water

Steps

  1. Marinate the chicken with yogurt, turmeric, cumin, coriander, garam masala, ginger, garlic, and salt. Let it sit for at least 30 minutes, or preferably overnight in the refrigerator.
  2. Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes, then drain.
  3. In a large pot, bring water to a boil and add salt. Cook the rice until it is 70% cooked (it should still have a bite to it). Drain and set aside.
  4. Heat oil or ghee in a large, heavy-bottomed pot. Add sliced onions and cook until golden brown.
  5. Add the marinated chicken to the pot and cook until it is no longer pink and starts to brown, about 10-15 minutes.
  6. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they are soft and blend into the chicken mixture.
  7. Add a bit of water if needed, and simmer until the chicken is fully cooked and the flavors are well combined.
  8. Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken mixture. Sprinkle fresh cilantro, mint leaves, and fried onions on top. Optionally, drizzle a few tablespoons of saffron milk over the rice.
  9. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together and the rice to finish cooking.
  10. Remove from heat and let it rest for a few minutes before gently fluffing the rice and serving.
  11. Garnish with additional fresh herbs, fried onions, and serve with raita or a side salad.
